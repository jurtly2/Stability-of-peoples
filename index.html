The Stability of Peoples

A guided rereading of The Wealth of Nations. We move slowly: exact Smith, plain Smith, comparison, then our answer.

Begin the reader → View doctrine so far

Plainly for all peoples

This doctrine is written plainly because it is meant for everyone, not only educated readers. If the ideas are for all peoples, the words must be reachable by all peoples.

The method

Each passage is split into layers so the reader never gets lost.

Exact Smith → Plain Smith → What he means → What we keep → What we challenge → Stability answer.

The agreement

We are not erasing Smith. We are rereading him. Preserve what helps life, expire what creates instability, and replace money-centered civilization with stability-centered civilization.

Why plain language matters

Smith’s exact wording stays visible, but every passage gets a plain version so workers, students, families, and serious readers can all understand the argument.

What prints

The print page exports only our doctrine so far. Smith’s text and comparisons remain in the reader, while the shareable PDF stays focused.

Psychological effects

People are changed by poverty, fear, isolation, trauma, scarcity, violence, and unstable surroundings. A stable society responds early when it can, helps after damage appears, and remembers that not everything can be predicted or controlled.

Fail-safe doctrine

Every idea must be tested. We show where Smith works, where Smith fails, where our answer works, and where our answer needs safeguards.
